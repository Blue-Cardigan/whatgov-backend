Tone: collaborative

Summary:
The parliamentary debate highlighted concerns over the adequacy of funding for the Money and Pensions Service, amid rising numbers of individuals requiring debt advice during the cost of living crisis.
Cross-party discussion emphasised the need for a reassessment of the financial levy and greater accountability for buy now, pay later lenders, with a call for enhanced visibility of financial services in local communities.
Ministerial commitments included ongoing review of funding levels and exploration of improved service accessibility, suggesting potential future policy adjustments.

Key Points:
Stella Creasy (Labour (Co-op), Walthamstow):
    Ms Stella Creasy asked the Minister to assess the adequacy of funding for the Money and Pensions Service, highlighting a potential underestimation of need in current models and suggesting further review.
    

Emma Reynolds (Labour, Wycombe):
    Emma Reynolds responded, stating that the funding for the Money and Pensions Service is regularly reviewed to meet demand, inflation, and changing needs.
    Context: Response to Stella Creasy's inquiry about funding adequacy.

Stella Creasy (Labour (Co-op), Walthamstow):
    Ms Stella Creasy referenced research indicating 7.5 million people lack necessary debt advice, attributed to a cost of living crisis and exclusion by the Money and Pensions Service of those behind on bills. She criticised the financial levy proposal and suggested a meeting to reconsider its funding model and further actions to regulate exploitative lenders.
    Context: Follow-up to initial query on funding adequacy, highlighting current service limitations.

Emma Reynolds (Labour, Wycombe):
    Emma Reynolds acknowledged Ms Creasy's concerns, noting ongoing reforms in 'buy now, pay later' sectors and recognising the gap in service accessibility. She mentioned that the Money and Pensions Service is exploring outreach initiatives to bridge these gaps.
    Context: Follow-up to Ms Stella Creasy's critique on service underestimation and levy issues.

Tim Farron (Liberal Democrat, Westmorland and Lonsdale):
    Tim Farron praised the Money and Pensions Service and similar organisation. for supporting people in debt, urging the Government to increase funding not only for staffing but also for community presence. He suggested leveraging post offices as venues for Government services, including financial advice.
    Context: Adding to discussion on funding and community visibility of financial support services.

Emma Reynolds (Labour, Wycombe):
    Emma Reynolds stated that the Money and Pensions Service partners with charities to help vulnerable populations and reiterated the constant review of its funding in light of ongoing demands.
