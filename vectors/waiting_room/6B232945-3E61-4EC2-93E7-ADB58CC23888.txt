Summary:
The discussion revolved around current job vacancies in the Prime Minister's Office, highlighting four positions open for application across the civil service and externally.
Notably, Sir Chris Wormald's recent appointment as the new Cabinet Secretary was acknowledged and welcomed amidst inquiries about other potential roles, such as the Prime Minister’s envoy to the nations and regions.
The dialogue remained light-hearted, with an exchange implying personal interests in the vacancies rather than a robust policy debate, maintaining a collaborative and humorous tone.

Key Points:
Lincoln Jopp (Conservative, Spelthorne):
    Lincoln Jopp inquired about current job vacancies in the Prime Minister's Office, specifically asking for details on advertised positions.
    

Ellie Reeves (Labour, Lewisham West and East Dulwich):
    Ellie Reeves responded by stating there are four advertised vacancies in the Prime Minister’s Office, all available either internally within the civil service or externally. She also noted the recent appointment of Sir Chris Wormald as the new Cabinet Secretary, extending congratulations to him.
    Context: Response to Lincoln Jopp's question regarding job vacancies.

Lincoln Jopp (Conservative, Spelthorne):
    Lincoln Jopp expressed pleasure in welcoming the new Cabinet Secretary and raised a follow-up query about the hiring for the role of Prime Minister’s envoy to the nations and regions, referencing Sue Gray’s decision not to take the job.
    Context: Response to Ellie Reeves regarding additional vacancies.

Ellie Reeves (Labour, Lewisham West and East Dulwich):
    Ellie Reeves made a light-hearted remark about Lincoln Jopp’s interest in Prime Minister's Office jobs, suggesting a private discussion if he was considering a career change.
    Context: Response to Lincoln Jopp's follow-up question about hiring for an envoy position.
