Title: Affordable Housing
Date: 2024-12-03 (Tuesday)
Type: Question
Location: Commons Chamber
House: Commons

Speakers:
- David Williams (Labour, Stoke-on-Trent North)
  Topics: Economy, Business, and Infrastructure
  Subtopics: Housing and Property Markets
- Rachel Reeves (Labour, Leeds West and Pudsey)
  Topics: Economy, Business, and Infrastructure
  Subtopics: Housing and Property Markets
- Gavin Williamson (Conservative, Stone, Great Wyrley and Penkridge)
  Topics: Economy, Business, and Infrastructure
  Subtopics: Housing and Property Markets

Tone: collaborative

Summary:
The government announced a significant increase in funding for affordable housing, allocating £500 million to boost the affordable homes programme, raising the annual budget to £3.1 billion.
Cross-party interest emerged in converting dormant historic buildings into affordable homes, with commitments from Labour MP Rachel Reeves to discuss this with relevant ministries.
Concerns were raised by Conservative MP Sir Gavin Williamson regarding reductions in right-to-buy discounts, prompting a response that such discounts would be reinvested in constructing new social housing.

Topics:
Economy, Business, and Infrastructure:
  Housing and Property Markets

Key Points:
David Williams (Labour, Stoke-on-Trent North):
    David Williams inquired about fiscal measures being taken by the government to increase the supply of affordable housing.
    

Rachel Reeves (Labour, Leeds West and Pudsey):
    Rachel Reeves stated that the government announced a £500 million boost to the affordable homes programme, raising the annual spend to £3.1 billion. Future grant investments will be detailed in phase 2 of the spending review.
    Context: Response to a question on fiscal measures for affordable housing.

David Williams (Labour, Stoke-on-Trent North):
    David Williams pointed out the existence of dormant historic buildings in Stoke-on-Trent and Kidsgrove, questioning what steps could be taken to convert them into affordable homes.
    Context: Follow-up on converting existing unused buildings into affordable housing.

Rachel Reeves (Labour, Leeds West and Pudsey):
    Rachel Reeves acknowledged David Williams' concern and mentioned that the £500 million boost allows up to 10% of affordable homes delivery to be sourced from acquiring existing homes, enabling social landlords to bring empty homes back into use for social housing.
    Context: Response to converting existing unused buildings into affordable housing.

Gavin Williamson (Conservative, Stone, Great Wyrley and Penkridge):
    Sir Gavin Williamson expressed concern over the reduction of right-to-buy discounts in Staffordshire to £16,000, advocating for the decision to be reviewed to help more people achieve home ownership.
    Context: Concern over changes to the right-to-buy discounts.

Rachel Reeves (Labour, Leeds West and Pudsey):
    Rachel Reeves confirmed that right-to-buy discounts will continue and emphasised that all proceeds will be reinvested into building new social housing, aiming to increase home ownership for more families.
    Context: Response to concerns about right-to-buy discounts and home ownership rates.
