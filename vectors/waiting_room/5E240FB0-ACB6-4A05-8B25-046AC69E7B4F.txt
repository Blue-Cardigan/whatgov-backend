Location: Grand Committee
House: Lords

  Topics: Economy, Business, and Infrastructure

Tone: neutral

Summary:
The UK government introduced regulations to enhance the Financial Services and Markets Act 2023, allowing specific legislation to be temporarily modified within financial market sandboxes to encourage technological innovation.
In a balanced discussion, all parties expressed support for the sandbox approach, acknowledging it as a creative regulatory tool, although concerns were raised regarding potential legislative gaps and the effectiveness of regulatory oversight in rapidly evolving sectors.
The government committed to maintaining robust regulatory standards while fostering innovation, outlining plans for future legislation adjustments based on technological developments and potential economic opportunities.

Topics:
Economy, Business, and Infrastructure:
  Trade and Industry, Banking and Financial Services, Competition Policy

Key Points:
Lord Livermore (Labour, Life peer):
    Lord Livermore introduces the Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 (Addition of Relevant Enactments) Regulations 2024. This regulation adds new 'enactments' to support financial market infrastructure sandboxes, allowing temporary legislative modifications for testing new technologies in financial markets. The Regulation covers digital securities and a new PISCES sandbox.
    
Keywords: Financial Services and Markets Act, digital securities sandbox, PISCES sandbox, temporary legislative modifications, financial market infrastructure

Baroness Kramer (Liberal Democrat, Life peer):
    Baroness Kramer supports sandboxes for financial innovations but raises concerns about the complexity and implications of regulations like PISCES, and questions if the government is proactive or reactive regarding digital financial growth and regulation. She highlights potential weaknesses in regulatory strategies and emphasises the importance of effective whistleblowing.
    Context: Response to Lord Livermore's introduction of the regulations.
Keywords: regulatory sandbox, PISCES, regulation weaknesses, whistleblowing, digital financial services

Lord Altrincham (Conservative, Excepted Hereditary):
    Lord Altrincham emphasises the importance of the FMI sandbox for innovation and economic growth, linking the regulatory framework change to post-Brexit opportunities. He highlights the inclusion of specific regulations in the sandbox regime aimed at maintaining high standards and innovating in areas like digital assets and green finance.
    Context: Response to the discussion of the new regulations.
Keywords: FMI sandbox, post-Brexit regulation, digital assets, economic growth, green finance

Lord Livermore (Labour, Life peer):
    Lord Livermore addresses concerns by assuring that regulations will achieve the same outcomes without watering down enforcement. He clarifies the timeline for PISCES and other sandbox initiatives, reaffirming the regulatory framework's adaptability. He also discusses the diligent anti-money laundering measures applied to new sandbox activities.
    Context: Response to questions and concerns raised by Baroness Kramer and Lord Altrincham.
Keywords: regulatory flexibility, PISCES timeline, anti-money laundering, sandbox activities, Financial Services and Markets Act
