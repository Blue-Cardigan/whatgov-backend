Tone: contentious

Summary:
Labour's Ian Murray highlighted a record £4.9 billion in Barnett consequentials intended to bolster Scotland's public services, yet criticised the SNP for voting against the Finance Bill from which these funds derive.
He stressed that these resources could address critical issues such as NHS waiting times and educational advancement, encouraging the Scottish Government to replicate UK Budget decisions like rates relief for hospitality, retail, and leisure sectors.
In a notable exchange, SNP members voiced concerns about central government funding decisions, while also calling for mirrored pensioner benefits across the UK, signifying a contentious cross-party dynamic over fiscal distribution and policy priorities.

Topics:
Economy, Business, and Infrastructure:
  Fiscal Policy and Public Spending, Trade and Industry, Regional Development, Housing and Property Markets

Key Points:
Ian Murray (Labour, Edinburgh South):
    Ian Murray stated that the relationship with the Scottish Government has been reset and mentioned multiple meetings with the Deputy First Minister to discuss the autumn Budget. He highlighted that Scotland benefited by £4.9 billion in Barnett consequentials from the UK Budget, criticising the SNP for voting against it while still using the funds.
    Context: In response to questions about discussions with the Scottish Government on the autumn Budget 2024 from Frank McNally and Irene Campbell.

Frank McNally (Labour, Coatbridge and Bellshill):
    Frank McNally inquired about guarantees from the Scottish Government to pass on the extra Barnett consequentials related to non-domestic rates in the Scottish Budget, emphasising the importance of a thriving retail, hospitality, and leisure sector to Scotland's brand. He expressed concern that failure to pass on these funds would damage the sector.
    Context: Following Ian Murray’s update on financial discussions and Barnett consequentials related to the autumn Budget.

Ian Murray (Labour, Edinburgh South):
    Ian Murray affirmed the importance of passing on rates relief to support growth and investment in high streets, and noted the investment from the UK Budget, including £200 million in the towns fund. He stressed that the Scottish Government should use their budget to deliver growth and support.
    Context: In response to Frank McNally's question about Barnett consequentials and their use in Scotland.

Irene Campbell (Labour, North Ayrshire and Arran):
    Irene Campbell emphasised the historic size of the budget provided to the Scottish Government through devolution, worth over £4.9 billion. She urged that the SNP should use this funding to improve public services as there are no longer excuses for them.
    Context: Continuing the discussion on the financial implications of the autumn Budget for Scotland.

Ian Murray (Labour, Edinburgh South):
    Ian Murray reiterated that the funds allocated to Scotland from the UK Government, amounting to billions beyond Barnett consequentials, were opposed by SNP MPs, although they would still use them. He criticised SNP MPs for voting against the Finance Bill yet benefiting from its provisions and mentioned the new Great British Energy initiative located in an SNP leader's constituency.
    Context: Responding to Irene Campbell's statement and highlighting SNP MPs' contradictory actions regarding UK Budget provisions.

Brendan O'Hara (Scottish National Party, Argyll, Bute and South Lochaber):
    Brendan O'Hara highlighted that the Scottish Government is introducing a pension-age winter heating payment for all Scottish pensioners and urged the Labour Government to provide similar support to pensioners in England and Wales who have lost their winter fuel allowance.
    Context: Introducing new Scottish Government measures for pensioners in the context of the discussed budgets and public spending.

Ian Murray (Labour, Edinburgh South):
    Ian Murray clarified Brendan O'Hara's point by stating that winter fuel payments in Scotland are devolved and managed by the Scottish Government.
    Context: Responding to Brendan O'Hara's comments about pension support and Scottish Government actions.

Pete Wishart (Scottish National Party, Perth and Kinross-shire):
    Pete Wishart raised concerns that the Budget resulted in the removal of £5 million in regeneration funding for Perth city centre. He criticised the UK Government's limited contribution from the levelling-up fund and questioned their treatment of Perth.
    Context: Expressing discontent with the funding allocations from the UK Government, particularly in relation to Perth.

Ian Murray (Labour, Edinburgh South):
    Ian Murray assured that no projects have been cancelled, specifically in Perth, but mentioned that the matter is under consideration by the relevant UK Government departments. He suggested that Pete Wishart take up the issue with his SNP colleagues, given the additional £4.9 billion in Barnett consequentials available.
    Context: Replying to Pete Wishart's complaint about funding cuts in the city of Perth.
