Summary:
The House of Lords has given approval to the draft Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 (Addition of Relevant Enactments) Regulations 2024, marking a step forward in updating relevant financial legislation.
Moved by Labour's Lord Livermore, the motion passed without recorded opposition, suggesting cross-party acceptance of the regulatory changes proposed in October.
The swift approval indicates parliamentary support for adjustments in financial services regulation, which may imply smoother implementation of future regulatory frameworks.

Lord Livermore (Labour, Life peer):
    Lord Livermore, a Labour life peer, formally moved for the approval of the draft Regulations related to the Financial Services and Markets Act 2023, which were laid before the House on 31 October. This motion had been considered in the Grand Committee on 2 December prior to this debate.
