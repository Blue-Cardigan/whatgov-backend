Key Points:
Ian Roome (Liberal Democrat, North Devon):
    Ian Roome asks the Prime Minister about assurances for North Devon district hospital to receive funding under the new hospital programme, citing its age, remote location, and limited capacity to serve a large population.
    Context: Following a welcome and engagement statement by Sir Keir Starmer.

Keir Starmer (Labour, Holborn and St Pancras):
    Sir Keir Starmer states the new hospital programme aims to address past failures and is committed to delivering projects such as the North Devon hospital, with details forthcoming from the Health Secretary.
    Context: In response to Ian Roome's inquiry about North Devon hospital funding.

Olivia Bailey (Labour, Reading West and Mid Berkshire):
    Olivia Bailey commends the government's actions on border security, highlighting a deal with Iraq to tackle smuggling gangs, and seeks agreement from the Prime Minister on international cooperation being key to ending smuggling.
    

Keir Starmer (Labour, Holborn and St Pancras):
    Sir Keir Starmer agrees with Olivia Bailey, noting the government's plan involves increased border security spending and cooperation, pointing out a significant number of returns of those with no right to remain in the UK.
    Context: Response to Olivia Bailey's question on border security and international coordination.

Kemi Badenoch (Conservative, North West Essex):
    Kemi Badenoch questions the Prime Minister about appointing a convicted fraudster as Transport Secretary, prompting accountability and transparency on immigration and other Westminster issues.
    

Keir Starmer (Labour, Holborn and St Pancras):
    Sir Keir Starmer rebuts Kemi Badenoch's focus on Westminster issues by emphasising past government immigration records and reminding her of his commitment to economic recovery and tackling systemic failures left by the previous government.
    Context: In response to Kemi Badenoch's accusations regarding his Transport Secretary appointment.

Ed Davey (Liberal Democrat, Kingston and Surbiton):
    Ed Davey raises the issue of reforming the electoral system for a fairer and more proportional representation, referencing a House vote favoring a bill for such reform, and seeks the government to allocate time for a debate.
    

Keir Starmer (Labour, Holborn and St Pancras):
    Sir Keir Starmer responds to Ed Davey by stating proportional representation is not Labour policy and government time will not be allocated for the bill's consideration.
    Context: In response to Ed Davey's request for proportional representation debate time.

David Williams (Labour, Stoke-on-Trent North):
    David Williams emphasises the need for certainty and action on the Middlewich eastern bypass, highlighting its potential economic impact, and seeks an early Christmas assurance from the Prime Minister.
    

Keir Starmer (Labour, Holborn and St Pancras):
    Sir Keir Starmer assures David Williams of the government's commitment to vital infrastructure, mentioning current assessments and offers to facilitate a meeting with the roads Minister for further discussion.
    Context: Response to David Williams regarding the Middlewich eastern bypass project.
