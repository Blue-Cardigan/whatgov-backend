Summary:
Labour MP Ashley Dalton highlighted the critical need for updated swimming facilities in West Lancashire amid challenges posed by ageing infrastructure and financial pressures exacerbated by recent economic crises.
Cross-party consensus emphasised the importance of accessible leisure services for community well-being, with calls for sustainable local government funding to mitigate the financial impact on pools and leisure centres.
Minister Stephanie Peacock underscored ongoing government investment in regional sports infrastructure and collaborations aimed at integrating health and leisure sectors to enhance community health outcomes.

Key Points:
Ashley Dalton (Labour, West Lancashire):
    Ashley Dalton highlighted the significance of swimming in West Lancashire and the importance of local swimming pools, Park Pool in Ormskirk and Nye Bevan Swimming Pool in Skelmersdale. She noted their long-term community service but emphasised their age and the urgent need for refurbishment or replacement due to modern demands.
    

Jim Shannon (Democratic Unionist Party, Strangford):
    Jim Shannon supported Ashley Dalton's emphasis on the importance of swimming facilities, linking them to health, leisure, social interaction, and overall wellbeing.
    Context: Following Ashley Dalton's initial points about the importance of swimming and local facilities.
Supported by: Ashley Dalton (Labour, West Lancashire)

Ashley Dalton (Labour, West Lancashire):
    Ashley Dalton elaborated on the financial challenges faced by West Lancashire in maintaining and replacing its swimming pools, noting increased project costs from £36.6 million to £49 million due to external factors. She stressed that current finances constrain the council's ability to fund large capital projects and highlighted the particular impact of swimming pool closures in deprived areas.
    Context: In response to Jim Shannon's support and to further discuss financial challenges.

Clive Jones (Liberal Democrat, Wokingham):
    Clive Jones mentioned his own experience with leisure facility development in Wokingham and underscored the importance of government funding for local authorities to support leisure centres amidst rising operational costs. He suggested that proper funding could prevent service cuts and facilitate better health outcomes and community cohesion.
    Context: Adding to Ashley Dalton's emphasis on financial challenges facing local leisure services.

Ashley Dalton (Labour, West Lancashire):
    Ashley Dalton reiterated the necessity for restructuring local government funding to support essential public amenities like swimming pools. She highlighted disparities in swimming proficiency among children from different socio-economic backgrounds and called for government intervention to maintain affordable public leisure facilities.
    Context: Responding to Clive Jones and continuing her appeal for government support.

Stephanie Peacock (Labour, Barnsley South):
    Stephanie Peacock supported Ashley Dalton's case for leisure facilities and acknowledged the pressing financial issues faced by West Lancashire Council. She assured that these concerns would be communicated to relevant government departments and highlighted the Department for Education's collaboration with organisation. to support swimming education as part of the curriculum. She also noted Sport England's role in funding developments and highlighted successful examples of community-focused, integrated leisure facilities enhancing public health outcomes.
    Context: In response to Ashley Dalton's request for government support and elaborating on department activities that could address the issues.
Supported by: Ashley Dalton (Labour, West Lancashire)
