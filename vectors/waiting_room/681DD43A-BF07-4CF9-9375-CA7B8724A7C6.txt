Tone: contentious

Summary:
The debate in the House of Lords centred on the UK's food security, highlighting a £5 billion government commitment to agriculture and £60 million in support for farmers affected by extreme weather, showcasing a significant investment in sustainable farming practises.
Despite these commitments, concerns were raised regarding the implications of tax reforms on family farms and the potential increase in corporate land acquisitions, with specific criticisms directed at the expansion of solar farms on agricultural land and the impact of these measures on food security.
Baroness Hayman indicated that discussions with stakeholders are ongoing and the broader national impact on food security is being assessed, alongside increased allocations for international development aid to address food insecurity abroad, suggesting a comprehensive approach to the issue.

Key Points:
Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    The Labour Government has committed £5 billion to the agricultural budget over the next two years. This includes an increase in the budget for environmental land management schemes to £1.8 billion in 2025-26, aimed at boosting food security and sustainable farming. Additionally, £60 million is allocated to support farmers affected by extreme wet weather last winter.
    
Keywords: agricultural budget, food security, sustainable farming, extreme weather support

The Lord Bishop of Hereford (Bishops, Bishops):
    The Lord Bishop of Hereford raised concerns that the changes to Agricultural Property Relief (APR) and Business Property Relief (BPR) could significantly reduce investment in agriculture. He requested an impact assessment of these tax changes on investment and productivity.
    
Keywords: APR impact, investment reduction, productivity, tax changes

Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Hayman of Ullock stated that reforms to APR aim to protect small family farms and food security. She mentioned ongoing discussions and committed to relay concerns to the Farming Minister.
    Context: In response to The Lord Bishop of Hereford's concerns about APR and BPR impact on investment.
Keywords: APR reforms, family farms, food security, Farming Minister

Lord Forsyth of Drumlean (Conservative, Life peer):
    Lord Forsyth of Drumlean questioned whether Defra was consulted adequately about APR changes, criticising the impact on small farms, as they might be forced to sell off to corporates. He also criticised government policy on solar farms on agricultural land, stating it undermines food security.
    Context: Following discussions about APR's impact on small farms and food security.
Keywords: APR consultation, small farms, corporate acquisition, solar farms, food security

Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Hayman of Ullock countered that solar farms are not being built on high-quality agricultural land and that discussions between Defra and the Treasury considered spending review plans, aiming to support long-term food security.
    Context: In rebuttal to Lord Forsyth of Drumlean's critique regarding solar farms and APR impacts.
Keywords: solar farms policy, Defra discussions, food security, land quality

Lord Browne of Ladyton (Labour, Life peer):
    Lord Browne of Ladyton highlighted that only 4% of the ODA budget was allocated to agricultural assistance under the previous government. He asked if this percentage will increase to address food insecurity linked to climate change.
    
Keywords: ODA budget, agricultural assistance, food insecurity, climate change

Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Hayman of Ullock confirmed an increase in the FCDO’s ODA budget to £9.24 billion by 2025-26, with a focus on practical support for communities facing hunger, including a £70 million resilience and adaptation fund for food-insecure areas.
    Context: In response to Lord Browne's question on increasing ODA for agriculture.
Keywords: ODA increase, resilience fund, hunger support, climate finance

Lord Alton of Liverpool (Crossbench, Life peer):
    Lord Alton of Liverpool brought up the concerning report about Uighur slave labour in tomato production imported to the UK, mislabeled as Italian. He called for restrictions on wrongly labeled goods to empower consumer choice.
    
Keywords: Uighur labour, import labeling, consumer choice, slave labour

Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Hayman of Ullock acknowledged the serious nature of the reports about Uighur slave labor and confirmed that supermarkets have denied involvement. The government is considering better labeling to inform consumers about product origins.
    Context: In response to Lord Alton's point about slave labor in food imports.
Keywords: import cheques, product labeling, consumer information, Uighur allegations

Lord Roborough (Conservative, Excepted Hereditary):
    Lord Roborough stated that 75% of British farming output comes from family farms now subject to family farm inheritance tax, stressing that this could undermine food security and increase food prices amid volatile farming conditions.
    
Keywords: family farms, inheritance tax, food security, farming volatility

Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Hayman of Ullock reiterated that changes to APR are not intended to harm small family farms and that discussions with stakeholders continue to address these issues.
    Context: In response to Lord Roborough's concerns about family farm taxation.
Keywords: APR discussions, family farm support, stakeholder meetings

Baroness Janke (Liberal Democrat, Life peer):
    Baroness Janke raised concerns about food bank dependency among families leading to nutritional deficiencies, especially affecting 800,000 children. She questioned the government's assessment of the impact on child health and development.
    
Keywords: food banks, child nutrition, dependency impact, child health

Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Hayman of Ullock emphasised the importance of child nutrition, mentioning collaborative work with schools on breakfast clubs and cross-departmental efforts for comprehensive policy support.
    Context: In response to Baroness Janke's point on food banks and child health.
Keywords: child nutrition, breakfast clubs, cross-departmental work, food policy

Lord Harris of Haringey (Labour, Life peer):
    Lord Harris of Haringey noted that the national risk register barely mentions food security, despite numerous challenges such as food supply weaponization, transport disruptions, and climate impacts. He queried plans to address these threats comprehensively.
    
Keywords: food security risks, national risk register, threat assessment, food supply chain

Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Hayman of Ullock assured that the government reviews household food security, referencing the Family Resources Survey, which noted a slight increase in food insecurity from 2020 to 2023.
    Context: Answering Lord Harris's inquiry about food security mentions in the risk register.
Keywords: household food security, Family Resources Survey, food insecurity

Baroness Rock (Conservative, Life peer):
    Baroness Rock inquired about discussions on solar planning applications affecting tenant farmers, where landlords might evict tenants for solar installations, asking about protection measures for tenants.
    
Keywords: tenant farmers, solar energy, landlord eviction, tenant protection

Baroness Hayman of Ullock (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Hayman of Ullock explained ongoing discussions with different departments regarding solar panel placements to balance energy and agricultural needs. The government plans to appoint a tenant farming commissioner as part of strengthening protections.
    Context: In response to Baroness Rock's concerns about tenant farmers and solar planning.
Keywords: solar planning, tenant farming commissioner, land use policy, farmer protections
