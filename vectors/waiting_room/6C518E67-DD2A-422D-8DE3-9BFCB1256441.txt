Title: Employer National Insurance: Hospices
Date: 2024-12-04 (Wednesday)
Type: Question
Location: Commons Chamber
House: Commons

Speakers:
- Seamus Logan (Scottish National Party, Aberdeenshire North and Moray East)
  Topics: Healthcare and Social Welfare
  Subtopics: National Health Service (NHS), Social Care and Support Services
- Kirsty McNeill (Labour (Co-op), Midlothian)
  Topics: Healthcare and Social Welfare
  Subtopics: National Health Service (NHS), Social Care and Support Services

Tone: contentious

Summary:
The debate centred on the impact of proposed employer national insurance contributions on Scottish hospices, highlighting their vulnerable financial situation.
There was an acknowledgment of the hospice sector's importance across party lines, yet criticisms were directed at both UK and Scottish governments for their handling of funding allocations.
The discussion saw Labour emphasising the Scottish government's funding responsibilities amid increased financial resources, while SNP raised concerns about the financial burdens imposed by policy changes.

Topics:
Healthcare and Social Welfare:
  National Health Service (NHS), Social Care and Support Services

Key Points:
Seamus Logan (Scottish National Party, Aberdeenshire North and Moray East):
    Seamus Logan asked about discussions with the Chancellor of the Exchequer regarding the impact of proposed changes to employer national insurance contributions on hospices in Scotland.
    

Kirsty McNeill (Labour (Co-op), Midlothian):
    Kirsty McNeill acknowledged the importance of hospices and stated that support for increased employer national insurance contributions will be allocated to Departments with the Barnett formula applied to devolved Governments. She mentioned that further details will be provided, with the rise starting in April.
    Context: In response to Seamus Logan's question about NI changes affecting hospices.

Seamus Logan (Scottish National Party, Aberdeenshire North and Moray East):
    Seamus Logan highlighted financial difficulties faced by Scottish hospices, mentioning Marie Curie's £3 million cost due to government decisions. He questioned what support will be provided to hospices like Marie Curie.
    

Kirsty McNeill (Labour (Co-op), Midlothian):
    Kirsty McNeill responded by attributing funding pressures on hospices to declines in real-term funding from the Scottish Government, despite the extra £5 billion funding secured by Scottish Labour MPs. She criticised the SNP for voting against this funding, implying it should enhance funding decisions for hospices in Scotland.
    Context: Responding to financial concerns raised by Seamus Logan regarding hospice funding.
