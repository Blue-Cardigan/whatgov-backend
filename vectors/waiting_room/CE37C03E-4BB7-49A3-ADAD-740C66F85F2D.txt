Summary:
The House of Lords has approved the Voter Identification (Amendment of List of Specified Documents) Regulations 2024, following a motion moved by Lord Khan of Burnley from the Labour party.
The regulation, previously reviewed by the Secondary Legislation Scrutiny Committee, was considered in a Grand Committee meeting before being brought to the Lords chamber.
The approval process concluded without noted objections, indicating that the motion passed smoothly and efficiently in the Lords.

Topics:
Parliamentary Affairs and Governance:
  Legislative Process

Key Points:
Lord Khan of Burnley (Labour, Life peer):
    Lord Khan of Burnley moved to approve the draft Regulations laid before the House on 15 October 2023, which pertain to amending the list of specified documents for voter identification. This motion was considered by the Secondary Legislation Scrutiny Committee in its 5th Report and debated in the Grand Committee on 28 November 2023.
