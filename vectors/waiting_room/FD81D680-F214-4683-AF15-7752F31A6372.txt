Title: Cyber-security
Date: 2024-12-05 (Thursday)
Type: Question
Location: Commons Chamber
House: Commons

Speakers:
- Nick Smith (Labour, Blaenau Gwent and Rhymney)
  Topics: Science, Technology, and Innovation
  Subtopics: Digital Infrastructure and Cybersecurity, Technology Education and Skills
- Pat McFadden (Labour, Wolverhampton South East)
  Topics: Science, Technology, and Innovation
  Subtopics: Digital Infrastructure and Cybersecurity, Technology Education and Skills

Tone: collaborative

Summary:
The UK government is set to introduce a cyber-security and resilience bill, underlining its priority on strengthening national cyber-defences as announced in the recent King's Speech.
Concerns were raised about the growing gap between existing cyber-defences and emerging threats, highlighting an urgent need to expand the workforce skilled in cyber-security as pointed out by the opposition.
Minister Pat McFadden emphasised collaborative efforts involving education, business, and critical infrastructure to bolster cyber-resilience, stressing an ongoing commitment to address both state and non-state cyber threats.

Topics:
Science, Technology, and Innovation:
  Digital Infrastructure and Cybersecurity, Technology Education and Skills

Key Points:
Pat McFadden (Labour, Wolverhampton South East):
    Pat McFadden stated that efforts are underway to enhance the UK's cyber-resilience, acknowledging the continual need for improvement. The Government plans to introduce a Cyber Security and Resilience Bill as part of the King's Speech, aiming to bolster the UK's cyber-defences and industry collaboration.
    

Nick Smith (Labour, Blaenau Gwent and Rhymney):
    Nick Smith highlighted a successful cyber security course at Ebbw Vale college and expressed concerns about a "widening gap" between the UK's cyber defences and external threats, as noted by the national cyber-security chief. He questioned what measures are being taken to expand the workforce necessary to protect critical infrastructure.
    Context: Follow-up inquiry from Pat McFadden's statement on cyber-security efforts.

Pat McFadden (Labour, Wolverhampton South East):
    Pat McFadden acknowledged Nick Smith's mention of the Ebbw Vale cyber-security course, reinforced the seriousness of threats from state and non-state actors, and underscored the commitment to collaborating with education, business, and critical national infrastructure to enhance protection measures. He emphasised the need to prioritise cyber-security alongside traditional military security.
    Context: Response to Nick Smith's question on workforce expansion and infrastructure protection.
