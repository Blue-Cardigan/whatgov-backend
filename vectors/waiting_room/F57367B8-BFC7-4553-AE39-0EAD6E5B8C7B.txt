Summary:
The House of Lords engaged in a detailed discussion about the UK government's housing targets and the involvement of regional mayors in strategic planning, highlighting a commitment to improve strategic planning mechanisms and cooperation with local authorities.
Ministerial commitments were made to address land-banking issues, enhance sustainable home construction standards, and publish the National Planning Policy Framework and English devolution White Paper by Christmas, emphasising a comprehensive approach to housing and devolved powers.
The debate identified cross-party concerns about achieving the home's target, emphasising the necessity of skilled workforce readiness, local input in planning, and innovative strategies like new towns, underscoring potential synergies between new town developments and regional mayoral powers.

Key Points:
Baroness Quin (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Quin inquired whether the government plans to hold discussions with regional mayors in England concerning house building targets.
    
Keywords: regional mayors, house building targets, discussions

Baroness Taylor of Stevenage (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Taylor of Stevenage affirmed the importance of strategic planning by mayors to address housing needs, emphasising the need for cooperation through established councils chaired by the Prime Minister and Deputy Prime Minister.
    Context: Response to Baroness Quin's question regarding government discussions with regional mayors.
Keywords: strategic planning, regional mayors, Council of the Nations and Regions

Baroness Quin (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Quin suggested that regional mayors have insights on where affordable housing is needed most and should be involved in the decision-making process to address issues like second homes on greenfield sites.
    Context: Continues her inquiry about regional mayor involvement in housing decisions.
Keywords: affordable housing, regional mayors, second homes

Baroness Taylor of Stevenage (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Taylor of Stevenage agreed with Baroness Quin, stating that mayors should be involved in strategic planning and that cooperation between authorities will be strengthened through the NPPF.
    Context: Response to Baroness Quin's suggestion about mayoral involvement.
Keywords: strategic planning, regional mayors, NPPF

The Earl of Courtown (Conservative, Excepted Hereditary):
    The Earl of Courtown raised concerns that housing targets will miss by 500,000 homes and questioned the government's commitment to these targets.
    
Keywords: housing targets, government commitment, shortfall

Baroness Taylor of Stevenage (Labour, Life peer):
    Baroness Taylor defended the government's housing efforts, asserting the need for affordable housing and criticising previous governments for inadequate action.
    Context: In response to The Earl of Courtown's inquiry about housing targets.
Keywords: affordable housing, government achievement, housing crisis
