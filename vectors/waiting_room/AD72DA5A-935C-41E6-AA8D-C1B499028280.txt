Title: Economic Growth in Rural Areas

Type: Question
Location: Commons Chamber
House: Commons

Speakers:

  Topics: Economy, Business, and Infrastructure

- Jo White (Labour, Bassetlaw)
  Topics: Economy, Business, and Infrastructure
  Subtopics: Fiscal Policy and Public Spending, Regional Development

Tone: collaborative

Summary:
Key topics included the government’s £5 billion investment in broadband connectivity and an equal amount for the farming budget aimed at enhancing rural economic growth.
Cross-party discussions highlighted concerns about business rates reform, with calls for a system that benefits small enterprises over large online retailers to stimulate high street and rural economy.
Despite differences in party perspectives, there is a clear ministerial commitment to support infrastructure and sustainable agriculture as unifying initiatives for rural progress.

Topics:
Economy, Business, and Infrastructure:
  Fiscal Policy and Public Spending, Regional Development, Transport and Infrastructure Development, Trade and Industry, Small Business Support

Key Points:
Jamie Stone (Liberal Democrat, Caithness, Sutherland and Easter Ross):
    Questions were raised about the government's fiscal actions to boost economic growth in rural areas.
    

Helen Morgan (Liberal Democrat, North Shropshire):
    Questions were asked about fiscal steps to encourage rural economic growth.
    

James Murray (Labour (Co-op), Ealing North):
    Investments of £5 billion in broadband and £5 billion for the farming budget are highlighted as steps to enhance rural economy growth through connectivity improvements and support for sustainable farming.
    Context: In response to questions on rural economic growth.

Jamie Stone (Liberal Democrat, Caithness, Sutherland and Easter Ross):
    There is a historical issue of highland depopulation which is being addressed with new projects like the green freeport and space launch in Sutherland, and a request was made for fiscal support for such ventures.
    

James Murray (Labour (Co-op), Ealing North):
    A light-hearted response indicated no commitment to visit the constituency, but acknowledged the attractiveness of the projects mentioned and scheduled to be in Scotland on unrelated business.
    Context: In response to Jamie Stone's invitation to visit and support fiscal initiatives.

Helen Morgan (Liberal Democrat, North Shropshire):
    There are issues with the current business rates system which disadvantages high street businesses compared to online retailers, and a call was made for a radical reform considering land values and investment incentives.
    

James Murray (Labour (Co-op), Ealing North):
    Plans to level the playing field between online and high street businesses through a permanent tax cut for certain sectors and an increased multiplier for properties with high rateable value were discussed. There was an invitation to contribute to a paper on business rate reforms.
    Context: In response to Helen Morgan's call for business rates reform.

Jo White (Labour, Bassetlaw):
    Concerns were raised about past government failures to protect British farmers, and a commitment was sought for better support in the future.
    

James Murray (Labour (Co-op), Ealing North):
    Acknowledgement was made of previous governmental failures to support farmers, and reassurance was given about ongoing support and investments, highlighting a £5 billion commitment to farming over the next two years.
    Context: In response to Jo White's concerns about government support for farmers.
