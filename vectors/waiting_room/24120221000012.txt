Title: Emergency Evacuation Consultation and Policy
Date: 2024-12-02 (Monday)
Type: Statement
Location: Written Statements
House: Commons

Speakers:
- Diana Johnson (Labour, Kingston upon Hull North and Cottingham)
  Topics: Legal Affairs and Public Safety
  Subtopics: Police and Emergency Services, Civil Rights and Liberties

Tone: neutral

Summary:
The UK Government has revealed its response to the consultation on emergency evacuation information sharing, unveiling a residential personal emergency evacuation plan policy aimed at enhancing fire safety in high-risk residential buildings.
This policy is a significant step in addressing the recommendations from the Grenfell Tower inquiry, highlighting the government's focus on improving safety for vulnerable groups living in high-rise flats.
The proposed regulations will soon be presented to Parliament for approval, marking a crucial progression towards greater safety measure enforcement.

Topics:
Legal Affairs and Public Safety:
  Police and Emergency Services, Civil Rights and Liberties

Key Points:
Diana Johnson (Labour, Kingston upon Hull North and Cottingham):
    The Government's response to the emergency evacuation consultation and the new residential personal emergency evacuation plan policy is being published. This policy focuses on improving fire safety for vulnerable residents in high-rise and higher-risk residential buildings. It is part of addressing the recommendations from the Grenfell Tower inquiry. The regulations to implement the policy will be laid out pending parliamentary approval. Documentation will be available in both Houses' Libraries and online.
    
Keywords: emergency evacuation, fire safety, Grenfell Tower inquiry, high-rise buildings, vulnerable residents
