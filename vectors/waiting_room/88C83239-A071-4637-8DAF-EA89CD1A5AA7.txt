Tone: contentious

Summary:
Labour Members emphasised their commitment to improving living standards through economic growth, a higher national living wage, and a child poverty taskforce, highlighting stark contrasts with the previous Conservative administration.
Significant financial commitments for Scotland were detailed, including historic block grants, underscoring Labour's strategy of investment over austerity, while pushing the Scottish Government to deliver effectively.
Conservative MPs challenged Labour's fiscal measures, especially the impact of increased national insurance contributions, but Labour rebutted by attributing low wage growth and high living costs to past Conservative policies.

Topics:
Economy, Business, and Infrastructure:
  Fiscal Policy and Public Spending, Regional Development, Employment and Labour Markets

Key Points:
Darren Jones (Labour, Bristol North West):
    Darren Jones highlighted plans to improve living standards by increasing economic growth and tackling child poverty, noting that living standards are expected to rise at over twice the previous rate during the current Parliament, as estimated by the Office for Budget Responsibility.
    

Natalie Fleet (Labour, Bolsover):
    Natalie Fleet pointed out the rise in child poverty during 14 years of Conservative governance, with one in five children nationally and a quarter in her constituency living in poverty. She demanded detailed measures from the Labour Government to address this issue.
    Context: Darren Jones responded on Labour's plans to improve living standards.

Darren Jones (Labour, Bristol North West):
    Darren Jones responded to Natalie Fleet, affirming Labour's commitment to improving living standards. He announced that the national living wage would rise by 6.7% to £12.21 per hour by April 2025, benefiting about 3 million low-paid workers. A taskforce on child poverty will report in 2025.
    Context: Natalie Fleet raised concerns about child poverty increases under Conservative government.

Pamela Nash (Labour, Motherwell, Wishaw and Carluke):
    Pamela Nash praised the Chancellor’s Budget for delivering substantial funds to Scotland, surpassing previous settlements. She distinguished Labour’s investment focus from the Tories and SNP, asking how it would benefit her constituents and all of Scotland.
    

Darren Jones (Labour, Bristol North West):
    Darren Jones highlighted the Scottish Budget's significant increase, with 20% more per person spending than the UK government's equivalent. He emphasised that it empowers the Scottish Government to deliver for the people of Scotland, with the SNP held accountable for their use of these resources.
    Context: Pamela Nash asked for details on the Scottish Budget's impact.

Graham Stuart (Conservative, Beverley and Holderness):
    Graham Stuart questioned the Minister about the employer NICs tax rise, claiming £19 billion comes from employees, with net Treasury gain only £11 billion after adjustments.
    

Darren Jones (Labour, Bristol North West):
    Darren Jones rebuffed Stuart, outlining the costs the public faced due to Conservative governance, including high inflation and energy costs. He emphasised Labour's corrective measures in public spending.
    Context: Graham Stuart inquired about the employer NICs tax rise.

Bradley Thomas (Conservative, Bromsgrove):
    Bradley Thomas challenged the Chancellor’s jobs tax, citing media admissions of its impact on lowering wages.
    

Darren Jones (Labour, Bristol North West):
    Darren Jones reiterated his commitment to economic growth to elevate living standards, suggesting Conservative members apologise for previous governance before critiquing Labour policies.
    Context: Bradley Thomas questioned the impact of the Chancellor's job tax on wages.
