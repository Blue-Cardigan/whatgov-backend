Summary:
The debate in Westminster Hall emphasised the importance of identifying and supporting children with incarcerated parents, a policy with cross-party backing.
Mr. Richard Holden and other Members highlighted existing systemic gaps, urging immediate action and recognition of vulnerable children, similar to support for families of the deployed military.
Ministerial responses affirmed a commitment to new initiatives and funding, while stressing the continuation of collaboration with various sectors to address these family needs comprehensively.

Key Points:
Roger Gale (Conservative, Herne Bay and Sandwich):
    Sir Roger Gale reminded members there is no opportunity for the member in charge to wind up due to the half-hour debate format.
    

Richard Holden (Conservative, Basildon and Billericay):
    Mr. Richard Holden initiated the debate by discussing the Government's commitment to supporting and identifying children of imprisoned parents, noting these children are at higher risk of engaging in crime. He cited a report stating half of prisoners have children 17 or younger, who are at risk of mental health issues and economic hardship. Oxfordshire data shows an increase in free school meals for these children after parental imprisonment.
    

Jim Shannon (Democratic Unionist Party, Strangford):
    Jim Shannon highlighted the challenges faced by vulnerable adult children of prisoners, stressing the need for available support.
    

Richard Holden (Conservative, Basildon and Billericay):
    Mr. Richard Holden acknowledged Jim Shannon's point on vulnerable adult children and emphasised the need for a child-focused approach in supporting children of prisoners, noting children often do not visit their imprisoned parent. He raised concerns about children left alone and stressed the importance of identifying and supporting these children through a national register.
    
Supported by: Jim Shannon (Democratic Unionist Party, Strangford)

Jake Richards (Labour, Rother Valley):
    Jake Richards agreed on the importance of supporting not only children but also their extended families, suggesting that family contact benefits the children and reduces reoffending.
    
Supported by: Richard Holden (Conservative, Basildon and Billericay)

Richard Holden (Conservative, Basildon and Billericay):
    Mr. Richard Holden concurred with Jake Richards on the importance of family involvement and highlighted the government’s step of making the first estimate of prisoners' children. He advocated for a system to identify and support these children individually, stressing the need for immediate action without lengthy consultations.
    
Supported by: Jake Richards (Labour, Rother Valley)

Alex Davies-Jones (Labour, Pontypridd):
    Alex Davies-Jones agreed with Mr. Holden and emphasised the impact of parental imprisonment on children’s immediate and long-term opportunities, supporting the need for aid to prevent generational disadvantages. She praised organisation. advocating for these children.
    
Supported by: Richard Holden (Conservative, Basildon and Billericay)

Cameron Thomas (Liberal Democrat, Tewkesbury):
    Cameron Thomas drew parallels between the impacts on children of prisoners and children of deployed armed forces personnel, suggesting similar support measures might be needed.
    

Alex Davies-Jones (Labour, Pontypridd):
    Alex Davies-Jones reiterated the importance of supporting children of prisoners, acknowledging similarities to other disrupted families. She emphasised the need to design support systems sensitive to each child’s needs.
    
Supported by: Cameron Thomas (Liberal Democrat, Tewkesbury)

Richard Holden (Conservative, Basildon and Billericay):
    Mr. Richard Holden appreciated the media's role in raising awareness of the issue and highlighted the need to remove stigma associated with having a parent in prison. He encouraged more openness in discussing the matter to help the public understand these challenges.
    Context: In response to Alex Davies-Jones's remarks on the media coverage.

Alex Davies-Jones (Labour, Pontypridd):
    Alex Davies-Jones described efforts by the Ministry of Justice to produce official statistics on children of prisoners, aiming to provide family support and strengthen parent-child relationships when appropriate. She detailed the Government's objectives to reach these children, provide necessary support, and improve family relations.
    

Alex Davies-Jones (Labour, Pontypridd):
    Alex Davies-Jones outlined specific governmental actions, including collaborations for better data sharing, support for parental imprisonment impacts, and plans for an extensive end-to-end support system. She mentioned integration with preventive services, doubling family help investment by 2025-26, and the work towards strengthening family relationships during imprisonment through various services.
    

Alex Davies-Jones (Labour, Pontypridd):
    Alex Davies-Jones detailed ongoing research and partnerships to address children of prisoners, ensuring support systems capture diverse needs. She highlighted roles in prisons to aid family ties and the ambition to reduce the female prison population, particularly concerning mothers.
