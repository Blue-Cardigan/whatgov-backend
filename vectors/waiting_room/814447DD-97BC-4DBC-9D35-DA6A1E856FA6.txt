Summary:
The House of Lords approved the draft 'Road Transport (International Passenger Services) (Amendment) Regulations 2024', presented by Labour's Lord Hendy of Richmond Hill.
This legislative update, discussed in Grand Committee, received endorsement following scrutiny from the Secondary Legislation Scrutiny Committee, illustrating a streamlined decision-making process.
The passage of these regulations signals a constructive approach to reforming international road transport legislation, with potential impacts on cross-border transport services.

Key Points:
Lord Hendy of Richmond Hill (Labour, Life peer):
    Lord Hendy of Richmond Hill moved to approve the draft Road Transport (International Passenger Services) (Amendment) Regulations 2024, which were presented to the House on 28 October. The regulations were previously considered in the Grand Committee on 2 December, following the 7th Report from the Secondary Legislation Scrutiny Committee.
