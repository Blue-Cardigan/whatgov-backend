Summary:
Labour's John Healey announced the development of a new defence industrial strategy aimed at integrating defence priorities with the UK's economic growth objectives.
The strategy outlines reforms to Ministry of Defence procurement and emphasises supporting Ukraine while fostering innovation, resilience, and increased exports within the defence sector.
This initiative reflects a cross-industry call for stakeholder consultation by February 2025, aligning defence efforts with the government's growth mission amid rising global security threats.

Key Points:
John Healey (Labour, Rawmarsh and Conisbrough):
    John Healey announced a new defence industrial strategy to be published in late spring 2025. This strategy aims to address the UK's growing national security threats, including Russia's invasion of Ukraine and tensions in various global regions. It seeks to reform Ministry of Defence procurement, support Ukraine, and develop industrial partnerships. The strategy will align with the Government's growth mission, promoting jobs and productivity in the UK. The strategy prioritises innovation, resilience, and market growth, with a focus on exports. Stakeholders are invited to consult on the published statement of intent by February 2025.
