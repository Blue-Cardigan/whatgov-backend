Tone: contentious

Summary:
During the Commons debate, Labour heralded its first budget in 14 years, emphasising a record £4.9 billion Barnett consequential for Scotland, and stressing an end to austerity with significant local investments including support for tech startups and clean energy projects.
The discourse revealed stark cross-party divisions, as Labour accused past Tory governments of reneging on funding promises and criticised national insurance increases, while Conservatives highlighted the adverse business sector feedback to the budget, including concerns from key industries like oil, hospitality, and agriculture.
Despite the contentious exchanges, Labour committed to prioritising economic growth, pledging investment in clean energy and university-incubated tech companies, illustrating a strategic focus on innovation and stability to bolster Scotland’s economic landscape.

Ian Murray (Labour, Edinburgh South):
    The Scotland Office is supporting the White Ribbon Scotland campaign, which aims to combat violence against women. Economic growth is prioritised, with a historic Budget for Scotland aimed at investment, featuring nearly £1.4 billion for local growth projects and initiatives like Great British Energy headquartered in Aberdeen.
    

Lillian Jones (Labour, Kilmarnock and Loudoun):
    The Labour Budget has allocated £4.9 billion for Scotland, marking the largest settlement since devolution and ending austerity. An additional £20 million has been dedicated to Kilmarnock, overcoming a £22 billion financial deficit left by previous government promises.
    Context: Ian Murray previously discussed economic growth and funding in Scotland.
Supported by: Ian Murray (Labour, Edinburgh South)
