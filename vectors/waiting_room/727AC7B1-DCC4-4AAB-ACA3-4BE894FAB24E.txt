Tone: contentious

Summary:
Debate in the House of Commons on transport connectivity highlighted significant partisan divisions, particularly regarding the development of the Borders railway line extension between Scotland and England.
While the Conservative MP John Lamont called for swift release of feasibility study funding, Minister Kirsty McNeill reiterated commitments to the Borderlands growth deal but stressed the ultimate responsibility lies with the Scottish Government.
The debate also exposed disagreements on air passenger duty policies, with contrasting views on how they impact regional connectivity and affordability, highlighting a broader contention over national transport priorities.

Key Points:
John Lamont (Conservative, Berwickshire, Roxburgh and Selkirk):
    John Lamont posed a question regarding the steps being taken to improve transport connectivity between Scotland and England.
    

Kirsty McNeill (Labour (Co-op), Midlothian):
    Kirsty McNeill acknowledged John Lamont as her predecessor and mentioned that both she and the Secretary of State would work with the Department for Transport and the Scottish Government to prioritise cross-border connectivity.
    Context: Response to John Lamont's question on transport connectivity.

John Lamont (Conservative, Berwickshire, Roxburgh and Selkirk):
    John Lamont highlighted the importance of extending the Borders railway line to Hawick, Newcastleton, and Carlisle for boosting jobs, the local economy, and social mobility. He expressed concern that the feasibility study funding agreed under the previous Conservative Government might be sidelined by the new Labour Government.
    Context: Follows up on previous statements, questioning progress on Borders railway extension study.

Kirsty McNeill (Labour (Co-op), Midlothian):
    Kirsty McNeill stated that the UK Government is committed to the Borderlands growth deal to deliver economic growth in Southern Scotland. She clarified that while the government was working on the business case for the Borders railway extension, the final delivery responsibility rested with Transport Scotland and the Scottish Government.
    Context: Response to concerns raised by John Lamont about the Borders railway project.

Andrew Bowie (Conservative, West Aberdeenshire and Kincardine):
    Andrew Bowie criticised the current government's decision to increase Air Passenger Duty, which he claims contradicts the goal of improving transport connectivity, particularly affecting people living far from London. He previously supported the decision to reduce the duty to make flights cheaper.
    Context: Raises issue of increased Air Passenger Duty affecting connectivity, connecting to previous government actions.

Kirsty McNeill (Labour (Co-op), Midlothian):
    Kirsty McNeill responded by framing current economic and climate challenges as inherited issues that necessitate difficult decisions, implying that financial constraints limit government action on connectivity.
    Context: Reply to Andrew Bowie's criticism of increased Air Passenger Duty.
