Summary:
The parliamentary debate spotlighted the UK's grave concerns over electoral irregularities in Georgia, reflecting on OSCE reports of voter intimidation and procedural violations, which threaten its Euro-Atlantic ambitions.
Cross-party agreement emphasised the importance of democratic norms, condemning police brutality against protesters and calling for Georgian authorities to investigate irregularities thoroughly.
The UK Government asserted a strategic pause in diplomatic engagement with Georgia, signalling a potential shift away from support unless democratic standards are restored, aligning with European partners' scrutiny.

Key Points:
Stephen Gethins (Scottish National Party, Arbroath and Broughty Ferry):
    Stephen Gethins asks the Secretary of State for Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Affairs for a statement on Georgia's parliamentary elections outcome.
    

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West expresses concerns over Georgia's elections, citing OSCE findings of voter secrecy breaches, procedural inconsistencies, and voter intimidation, damaging Georgia's reputation. She urges the Georgian Government to implement OSCE recommendations.
    Context: Response to Stephen Gethins' urgent question regarding Georgia's elections.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Georgia's anti-western rhetoric and legislative actions, such as the law on transparency and foreign influence, undermine UK-Georgia relations and democratic values. Excessive police force against protesters is condemned, and UK-related dialogues are paused.
    Context: Elaboration on UK concerns and response following the Georgian elections.

Stephen Gethins (Scottish National Party, Arbroath and Broughty Ferry):
    Stephen Gethins asks about discussions with European partners regarding Georgia's EU accession and how UK supports rule of law, media freedom, and sanction maintenance against Russia by Georgia.
    Context: Follows up on UK response to Georgia's political situation.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West emphasises media freedom and peaceful protest importance. UK calls for election irregularities investigations and pauses political level discussions awaiting stability and new government formation.
    Context: Responds to Stephen Gethins' inquiry about UK and European actions regarding Georgia.

Emily Thornberry (Labour, Islington South and Finsbury):
    Emily Thornberry refers to OSCE reports of election manipulation in Georgia and highlights the European Parliament's call for a re-election. She questions if the UK shares this stance.
    Context: Concerns about election fairness and alignment with European actions.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West clarifies the UK stance on monitoring the situation in Georgia, supporting thorough investigation of election irregularities without calling for immediate re-elections, as investigations are ongoing.
    Context: Response to Emily Thornberry's question on UK's election re-run position.

Wendy Morton (Conservative, Aldridge-Brownhills):
    Wendy Morton agrees with government concerns on Georgia, emphasising the need to support Georgian people's Euro-Atlantic aspirations and urges UK to discourage aggressive rhetoric and escalate diplomatic support for democracy.
    Context: Aligns with UK government's position on recent Georgian political developments.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West notes UK's support for Georgia's Euro-Atlantic aspirations and highlights ongoing UK efforts to ensure peaceful demonstrations and thorough investigations into election conduct. Reiterates UK's focus on soft power.
    Context: Answer to Wendy Morton's query about UK strategies and soft power in Georgia.

Joe Powell (Labour, Kensington and Bayswater):
    Joe Powell calls for potential sanctions against Georgian Dream officials with ties to the UK amidst reports of protesters and journalists being assaulted. He emphasises the need for freedom of the press enforcement.
    Context: Focus on accountability for human rights violations in Georgia.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West assures that current investigations into UK-Georgia links and potential sanctions will be considered but not disclosed prematurely.
    Context: Response to Joe Powell's request for sanctions and investigation of foreign influences in Georgia.

Calum Miller (Liberal Democrat, Bicester and Woodstock):
    Calum Miller questions UK's suspension of strategic dialogues with Georgia. He supports increased UK-EU cooperation to counter democratic decline and Russian influence in Georgia, and he seeks clarification on UK conditions for further partnership suspensions.
    Context: Explores UK's diplomatic stance and action thresholds in response to Georgia's political situation.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West outlines UK's current diplomatic stance due to Georgia's democratic backsliding, pausing certain dialogues while pushing for investigation outcomes before taking further actions. Reaffirms UK strategic alignments and possibility for future measures.
    Context: Response to Calum Miller's question on strategic suspension and bilateral relations.

John Whittingdale (Conservative, Maldon):
    Sir John Whittingdale highlights Russian election interference allegations in Georgia and Moldova, urging UK actions to support democratisation efforts and counter Russian influence.
    Context: Addresses regional security concerns amid Georgian election issues.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West condemns Georgian government actions against NGOs and media, emphasises monitoring election post-investigation, and mentions UK's response involving tactical suspensions and diplomatic engagements.
    Context: Follows up on Sir John Whittingdale's inquiry on UK support for democracy in Georgia.

David Chadwick (Liberal Democrat, Brecon, Radnor and Cwm Tawe):
    David Chadwick inquires about UK efforts to ensure protest rights in Georgia amidst election-related unrest.
    Context: Focuses on civil liberties and government response amid Georgia's political climate.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West calls for restraint from the Georgian police and ensures monitoring of civil society's ability to demonstrate freely. Acknowledges young people's desire for a democratic future and UK commitment to the same.
    Context: Response to David Chadwick's question on protecting protest freedoms.

Brendan O'Hara (Scottish National Party, Argyll, Bute and South Lochaber):
    Brendan O'Hara questions why the UK has not supported the European Parliament's call for re-running the Georgian elections. He seeks clarity on UK interactions with Georgian officials regarding election conduct repercussions.
    Context: Discusses UK's stance comparison with EU actions in response to Georgian elections.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West explains UK's conditional stance on Georgian election rerun calls, waiting for investigation outcomes while maintaining diplomatic influence. Highlights strategic dialogue suspensions pending review results.
    Context: Response to Brendan O'Hara's concerns on UK and EU alignment regarding Georgia.

Jim Shannon (Democratic Unionist Party, Strangford):
    Jim Shannon asks about UK support for democracy in Georgia, in light of Russian influences, and resources available to British citizens in Tbilisi during current unrest.
    Context: Addresses logistics and support mechanisms amidst Georgian political turmoil.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West assures the UK embassy in Tbilisi is well-equipped to assist and commit to Georgia's democratic progress while monitoring election irregularities. Reinforces UK's support for Georgia's EU aspirations and condemns police brutality.
    Context: Response to Jim Shannon's inquiry on UK engagement in Georgia.

Pippa Heylings (Liberal Democrat, South Cambridgeshire):
    Pippa Heylings voices concern over legislation in Georgia restricting freedoms and the anxiety it causes among young Georgians in the UK. She asks if the Minister will meet with them to offer support.
    Context: Concerns on impact of Georgian domestic policies on diaspora.

Catherine West (Labour, Hornsey and Friern Barnet):
    Catherine West acknowledges Pippa Heylings' concerns, supports peaceful political expression, and commits to relaying feedback to relevant parties. prioritises listening to observer findings from the election process to guide future actions.
    Context: Addresses support for impacted individuals and adherence to election monitoring outcomes.
