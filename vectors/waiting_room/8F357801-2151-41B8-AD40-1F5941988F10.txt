Tone: collaborative

Summary:
The debate centred on the urgent need for leasehold reforms, with both Labour and Conservative members highlighting the challenges faced by leaseholders due to high service charges and poor management practises.
Labour's Matthew Pennycook reiterated the government's commitment to end the leasehold system within this Parliament, emphasising the need for protective measures against unscrupulous agents and addressing the fallouts of previous legislation.
Cross-party engagements were evident, with Pennycook offering to meet Conservative and Labour MPs alike, signalling a collaborative tone aiming to ensure comprehensive reform implementation.

Topics:
Economy, Business, and Infrastructure:
  Housing and Property Markets

Key Points:
Matthew Pennycook (Labour, Greenwich and Woolwich):
    The Government is determined to end the feudal leasehold system as part of their manifesto commitments. Plans to implement reforms in the leasehold system are underway, with updates provided in a recent written ministerial statement.
    
Keywords: leasehold reforms, feudal system, government commitment, manifesto, written statement

Damien Egan (Labour, Bristol North East):
    Damien Egan shared feedback from residents in Fishponds, who face excessive leasehold charges from their property management company, FirstPort, and seeks assurance from the Government to eliminate misleading and unjust fees.
    
Keywords: Fishponds residents, excessive charges, leasehold abuse, FirstPort, government assurance

Matthew Pennycook (Labour, Greenwich and Woolwich):
    Matthew Pennycook acknowledges the problem of unjustified fees and charges imposed on leaseholders by managing agents. He emphasised the importance of protecting leaseholders from such abuses, mentioning the need to regulate unscrupulous agents.
    Context: Response to Damien Egan's concerns about excessive charges by FirstPort.
Supported by: Damien Egan (Labour, Bristol North East)
Keywords: unjustified charges, leaseholder protection, unscrupulous agents, regulation, homeownership

Julian Lewis (Conservative, New Forest East):
    Sir Julian Lewis raised concerns about additional apartment levels added to leaseholder blocks without changing planning permissions, and the unfair passing of extension costs to leaseholders.
    
Keywords: apartment extensions, planning permissions, extension costs, leaseholder burden, New Forest East

Matthew Pennycook (Labour, Greenwich and Woolwich):
    Matthew Pennycook agreed to discuss with Sir Julian Lewis ways to protect leaseholders from cost increases due to poor quality permitted developments post-2013.
    Context: In response to Sir Julian Lewis's concern about passing extension costs to leaseholders.
Supported by: Julian Lewis (Conservative, New Forest East)
Keywords: permitted development, leaseholder protection, cost increases, building quality, post-2013 developments

Marsha De Cordova (Labour, Battersea):
    Marsha De Cordova highlights high service charges faced by Battersea leaseholders and requests a meeting with the Minister to discuss Labour's leasehold protection plans.
    
Keywords: Battersea leaseholders, service charges, management agents, meeting request, Labour protection plans

Matthew Pennycook (Labour, Greenwich and Woolwich):
    Matthew Pennycook agrees to meet with Marsha De Cordova and her constituents, emphasising the need to balance reform speed with thoroughness. He references flaws in the Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024 as a cautionary example for careful reform.
    Context: Responding to Marsha De Cordova's request for a meeting about leaseholder protection plans.
Supported by: Marsha De Cordova (Labour, Battersea)
Keywords: leasehold reform, meeting agreement, reform speed, careful reform, Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024

Rebecca Smith (Conservative, South West Devon):
    Rebecca Smith welcomes leasehold reform commitment but raises concerns about service charges in new town developments like Sherford, seeking plans to address this issue.
    
Keywords: new town developments, service charges, leasehold reform, Sherford, residential freeholders

Matthew Pennycook (Labour, Greenwich and Woolwich):
    Matthew Pennycook responds to Rebecca Smith by acknowledging the issue of management estate charges and the need to reduce private and mixed-tenure housing estates, which contribute to service charge problems.
    Context: In response to Rebecca Smith's concerns about service charges in new developments.
Supported by: Rebecca Smith (Conservative, South West Devon)
Keywords: management estate charges, housing estates, service charge problems, private and mixed-tenure, leasehold reform
