Tone: collaborative

Summary:
The parliamentary debate highlighted a shift towards allocating local growth funding based on need instead of competitive bidding, with a commitment to introduce multi-year funding to better serve communities.
Labour MPs underscored the inefficiencies of the previous 'beauty parade' funding model, while acknowledging it resulted in misallocated resources and frustration among local authorities.
The engagement from opposition members and the Minister's assurance to collaborate with local governments across various tiers, including Northern Ireland, suggests a future framework for more tailored and effective local governance.

Key Points:
Rachel Taylor (Labour, North Warwickshire and Bedworth):
    Rachel Taylor asked about steps taken by the department to allocate local growth funding according to need, emphasising the wastefulness of making local authorities bid for central government funds and the importance of fair funding for local authorities to deliver necessary services.
    
Keywords: local growth funding, allocation needs, fair funding, local authorities, services delivery

Gill German (Labour, Clwyd North):
    Gill German inquired about ensuring national funding streams support local delivery, especially for seaside towns, while avoiding a one-size-fits-all approach, referencing the 2019 "Future of Seaside Towns" report.
    
Keywords: seaside towns, local delivery, funding streams, one-size-fits-all, growth strategy

Alex Norris (Labour (Co-op), Nottingham North and Kimberley):
    Alex Norris stated a shift towards allocated, multi-year funding settlements based on need, ending the competitive funding model to allow local leaders to drive growth in areas needing it most.
    
Supported by: Rachel Taylor (Labour, North Warwickshire and Bedworth)
Keywords: multi-year funding, allocation by need, local growth, competitive funding, growth strategy

Rachel Taylor (Labour, North Warwickshire and Bedworth):
    Rachel Taylor expressed concern over the previous inefficient "beauty parade" funding model, causing disappointment and not addressing real needs, looking for assurance of fair allocation under the current government.
    Context: Response to Alex Norris's explanation of multi-year funding.
Supported by: Alex Norris (Labour (Co-op), Nottingham North and Kimberley)
Keywords: beauty parade funding, efficiency, fund allocation, real needs, government policy

Alex Norris (Labour (Co-op), Nottingham North and Kimberley):
    Alex Norris emphasised the need for flexibility for local expertise to tailor funding to their needs, particularly in areas like Rhyl, advocating for breaking the one-size-fits-all funding approach.
    Context: Response to Gill German's question on local delivery.
Supported by: Gill German (Labour, Clwyd North)
Keywords: local expertise, flexibility, funding model, one-size-fits-all, community needs

Manuela Perteghella (Liberal Democrat, Stratford-on-Avon):
    Manuela Perteghella proposed that district and borough councils, as the closest form of government, should allocate UK shared prosperity funds due to their understanding of local needs.
    
Keywords: district councils, borough councils, shared prosperity fund, local government role, community needs

Alex Norris (Labour (Co-op), Nottingham North and Kimberley):
    Alex Norris responded to the concept of shared prosperity funding by highlighting the importance of collaboration across all tiers of local government, to improve communities.
    Context: Response to Manuela Perteghella's proposal regarding local government role in fund allocation.
Keywords: collaboration, local government, shared prosperity, community improvement, funding coordination

Jim Shannon (Democratic Unionist Party, Strangford):
    Jim Shannon raised concerns about Northern Ireland still awaiting shared prosperity funding and its potential benefits for addressing coastal issues like erosion. He inquired about discussions with the Northern Ireland Executive.
    
Keywords: Northern Ireland, shared prosperity funding, coastal erosion, government discussions, funding allocation

Alex Norris (Labour (Co-op), Nottingham North and Kimberley):
    Alex Norris reassured that discussions are ongoing with Northern Ireland's Executive and local authorities to determine shared prosperity funding allocations, acknowledging previous interactions and promising forthcoming details.
    Context: Response to Jim Shannon's concerns about funding for Northern Ireland.
Keywords: shared prosperity funding, Northern Ireland, funding discussions, allocation plans, local authorities
