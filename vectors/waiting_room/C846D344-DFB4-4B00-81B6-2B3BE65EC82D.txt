Title: Pimping websites and paying for sex
Date: 2024-12-04 (Wednesday)
Type: Petition
Location: Commons Chamber
House: Commons

Speakers:
- Carolyn Harris (Labour, Neath and Swansea East)
  Topics: Legal Affairs and Public Safety
  Subtopics: Criminal Justice System, Civil Rights and Liberties, Consumer Protection Law

Tone: neutral

Summary:
The debate centred on a petition urging the UK government to outlaw online platforms facilitating commercial sexual exploitation and to support trafficking victims.
Labour MP Carolyn Harris highlighted the role of technological advancement in enabling sex trafficking, advocating for regulations to keep pace with such changes.
The petition emphasises the need for punitive measures against pimping websites and financial transactions for sex, alongside supportive measures for victims, reflecting a growing concern over gender-based violence in society.

Topics:
Legal Affairs and Public Safety:
  Criminal Justice System, Civil Rights and Liberties, Consumer Protection Law

Key Points:
Carolyn Harris (Labour, Neath and Swansea East):
    Carolyn Harris presented a petition calling for the outlawing of pimping websites and paying for sex, highlighting that these websites facilitate the sexual exploitation of women and are a primary vehicle for sex trafficking. She stated that laws have not kept up with technological advancements, making these sites easily accessible and instrumental in trafficking crimes. Harris emphasised the vulnerability of trafficking victims and the necessity of supporting rather than sanctioning them.
