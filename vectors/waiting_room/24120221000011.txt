Summary:
The publication of the annual report by the Biometrics and Surveillance Camera Commissioner, highlighted in a statement by Dame Diana Johnson, marks a significant step in governmental transparency regarding surveillance practises.
As this is the final report by outgoing commissioner Tony Eastaugh, the focus now shifts to appointing a successor whose task will be to continue overseeing biometric and surveillance operations as mandated by the Protection of Freedoms Act 2012.
The report's availability online and in parliamentary offices ensures accessibility for further scrutiny and discussion among stakeholders concerned with privacy and surveillance issues.

Key Points:
Diana Johnson (Labour, Kingston upon Hull North and Cottingham):
    Dame Diana Johnson announced the publication of the Biometrics and Surveillance Camera Commissioner's annual report, covering April 2023 to March 2024. This report is crucial for understanding the exercise of statutory functions in surveillance and biometrics. The role is appointed by the Home Secretary under the Protection of Freedoms Act 2012, emphasising its statutory and independent nature. The report marks Tony Eastaugh's final contribution before his departure, with a new commissioner soon to be appointed. Full access to the report is available on gov.uk and the Vote Office.
